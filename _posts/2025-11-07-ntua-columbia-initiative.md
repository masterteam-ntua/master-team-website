We invite all of you to the kick-off event of the new **Lab to Market: Bridging Innovation and Entrepreneurship** program – an intensive training initiative on startup creation and technological innovation.

The program is co-organized by **NTUA**, **Columbia University**, **Endeavor Greece** and the **Hellenic Institute for Advanced Studies (HIAS)**, with the generous support of the **Blavatnik Family Foundation**.

During the event, students will learn about the structure of the program, the application process, the selection criteria, and the opportunities it offers for transforming research and ideas into real-world ventures.

### What the program offers

Participants will benefit from a comprehensive learning journey that includes:

- **Startup Bootcamp in Athens (14–16 January 2026)**  
  An intensive three-day bootcamp with hands-on workshops, mentoring and team-based learning, focused on international best practices in entrepreneurship.

- **Virtual Program (January–April 2026)**  
  Weekly online training sessions, mentoring and market/customer discovery activities with faculty and invited speakers from **Columbia University**, **NTUA**, **Endeavor Greece**, **HIAS** and the global innovation ecosystem.

- **Industry Mentorship & Networking**  
  Each team will be matched with experienced mentors and gain access to networking events with industry representatives and professionals from relevant sectors.

- **Incentives & International Exposure**  
  Top-performing participants will be offered a fully funded trip to **New York in June 2026** to present their projects and connect with the global innovation ecosystem. Winning teams in the final pitch competition will receive **financial awards** to further develop their ideas.

### Thematic focus

The main thematic areas of the program are:

- Artificial Intelligence  
- Energy & Sustainability  
- Health  

Outstanding proposals from other technological fields will also be considered.

### Who can apply

The program is open to **all NTUA students**, with priority given to:

- PhD candidates  
- Master’s students  
- Final-year undergraduate students  
- Post-doctoral researchers  

Applications are submitted in **teams of 2–4 members**. Exceptional individual applications may also be considered; if selected, applicants will be invited to form or join teams based on shared interests.

### Key dates

- **Application deadline:** 15 December 2025  
- **Program start (Bootcamp in Athens):** 14–16 January 2026  

Further details on the **kick-off event (date, time, venue)** and the **application procedure** will be announced shortly through the School’s channels and the program website.

We warmly encourage all interested students and researchers to attend the kick-off event and explore this exceptional opportunity to bring their ideas **from lab to market**.
